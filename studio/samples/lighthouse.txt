The Lighthouse Keeper

Chapter 1

The wind howled across the black rocks as Mara climbed the spiral stairs. The great lamp had gone dark for the first time in forty years, and she meant to know why before dawn.

"You shouldn't be up here alone," said Elias, following close behind. His voice was rough with worry.

"Someone has to be," Mara replied. "The ships won't wait for us to be brave."

Elias sighed. "Then at least let me carry the oil."

She laughed softly. "You never change, old friend."

At the top the lamp stood cold and silent. Mara touched the cracked glass and whispered, "We'll bring you back."

Chapter 2

By morning the storm had passed. Elias shouted from the gallery below, "Mara! The light — it's burning again!"

She said nothing. She only smiled, and watched the ships turn safely home.
